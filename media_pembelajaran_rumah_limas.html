Title: Rumah Limas, The Heritage of Palembang

[Identification]
Rumah Limas is the traditional house of Palembang, South Sumatra Province, Indonesia. It is a prominent symbol of the region's rich cultural heritage and is widely recognized for its unique architecture, which perfectly blends functionality with philosophical values.

[Description]
The house is fundamentally built on stilts (*rumah panggung*). This elevated design was historically intended to protect the inhabitants from floods, as Palembang is known for its rivers and swamps, and to avoid wild animals. The roof has a distinctive *limasan* (pyramid-like) shape, adorned with traditional ornaments called *simbar* which symbolise majesty and beauty.

Rumah Limas is primarily constructed using strong, high-quality local wood. *Kayu Ulin* (ironwood), known for its water-resistant properties, is utilized for the foundation and pillars. Meanwhile, *Kayu Tembesu* is used for the walls, floors, and doors. These woods make the structure extremely durable.

One of the most fascinating features of the interior is its terraced floor called *kekijing*. The house is divided into several levels (usually two to five levels). These levels represent the social status, age, or hierarchy of the guests. The first level is typically for ordinary guests, while the higher levels are strictly reserved for respected elders, community leaders, or close family members. Furthermore, the wooden walls and cabinets are intricately decorated with beautiful carvings featuring floral motifs, such as jasmine and lotus, combined with golden paint, showcasing the luxurious influence of the Sriwijaya empire.

  • Specific Participant: Rumah Limas.
  • Simple Present Tense: "The house is divided...", "It is a prominent symbol...".
  • Adjectives: prominent, unique, distinctive, strong, high-quality, beautiful, golden.
  • Passive Voice: "...is primarily constructed using...", "...are intricately decorated...".
